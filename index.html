Who we are

The Laboratory of Forest Sciences and Biodiversity Informatics (LSFBI) is one of the decentralized research units of the Faculty of Agricultural Sciences (FSA) of the University of Abomey-Calavi. Since October 2000, LSFBI focused on training and research in forest sciences and biodiversity informatics so as to provide to decision makers, demand-driven research products and skillful resource managers to support forest management and biodiversity conservation in the context of climate and global changes. The LSFBI was created as Laboratory of Forest Sciences (LSF) by the Dean of the Faculty of Agronomic Sciences by Order No. 538 – 12/UAC/FSA/D on July 6th, 2012.

What we do

The laboratory is made of a multidisciplinary team whose work is structured according to priority research axes:

  • Silviculture, structure, development and sustainable management of forests.

    • This research unit deals with silviculture work from the stage of plant production, soil preparation, planting, cultural care, forest development and logging in order to contribute to the conservation and sustainable management of forests.


  • Non-Timber Forest Products

    • This research unit deals with the ethno botanical study of valuable species, establishing the secular links of interest between the species and populations. The purpose is to raise awareness among populations about the conservation and sustainable use of valuable forest species.


  • Biodiversity Informatics and Biodiversity Conservation in the Context of Climate and Global Change

    • This research unit concerns the analysis of biodiversity data, in connection with climate change and environmental factors in general, to generate results to inform decision-making on biodiversity conservation in the context of climate and global change
